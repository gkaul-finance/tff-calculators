10 Interactive Calculators
These calculators are examples—illustrations of what can be built for specific numerical problems. They are not shortcuts. Calculators hide more than they reveal: a tool that hands you an answer can create the illusion of understanding where none exists. In practice, you will need to build your own models—in Excel, in code, or even on paper—and it is precisely in that building that you will understand what is really going on. The exercises in the book do not all require these calculators. Many are deliberately conceptual, because finance is ultimately about how you think, not how you calculate.
